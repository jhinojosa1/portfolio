Transportation infrastructure / Utility coordination

TxDOT I-35 · Georgetown, Texas

Project overview

Making hidden conflicts visible.

As a Subsurface Utility Engineer with Lockwood, Andrews & Newnam, I supported utility-conflict identification and relocation planning for the Georgetown section of TxDOT I-35.

This assignment was part of broader subsurface utility work spanning more than 20 TxDOT roadway corridors.

RoleSubsurface Utility Engineer
Documented500+ utility conflicts
DatesJun 2024–Apr 2025

01

The challenge

Proposed roadway improvements had to be coordinated against existing water, sewer, gas, electric, telecom, and drainage systems—each with different records, owners, and relocation constraints.

02

My responsibility

I reviewed plans, as-builts, and field-verification data to identify and document more than 500 conflicts for the Georgetown corridor and supported coordination among clients, utility owners, and designers.

03

The deliverable

I produced the utility-conflict matrix and project drawing illustrating all identified conflicts, giving the team a structured basis for relocation proposals and roadway-design coordination.
