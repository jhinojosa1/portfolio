Prototype / In development

AI Haul-Route Intelligence

I am building a vendor-independent telematics system that pairs GPS tracking with a 4G LTE connection to record dump-truck haul routes and cycles in the field. The planned analysis layer will use AI-assisted methods to compare travel paths, cycle times, stops, and route consistency—giving project teams better evidence for identifying efficient hauling patterns. The goal is useful fleet visibility and route intelligence without requiring a premium OEM productivity subscription.

Prototype / In development

Long-Range HaLow Field Network

I am prototyping a long-range IP network using Wi-Fi HaLow radios with Raspberry Pi Zero 2 W field nodes and a Raspberry Pi 4 gateway. An office-connected gateway can extend network access toward equipment in remote areas, while local nodes provide a connection for survey-data transfer, design uploads and downloads, and machine communication. The goal is to reuse existing site internet and reduce dependence on separate cellular plans or proprietary OEM connectivity services. Actual range will depend on terrain, antenna placement, interference, and line of sight.

Community initiative / Planning

Houston Community Resilience Mesh

I participate in the Meshtastic community and am working toward a network of affordable, renewable-powered LoRa nodes placed across Houston and, eventually, other large cities. Each node would relay short messages across the mesh without relying on cellular service or internet access, giving neighbors an additional way to communicate during hurricanes, floods, and other natural disasters. The design goal is a repeatable, low-cost node with solar power, battery backup, weather protection, and thoughtful siting that can scale through community participation. This network is intended to complement—not replace—911, public-safety radio, or official emergency-alert systems.
