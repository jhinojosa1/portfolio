Landfill construction / Coal-ash pond closure

Clifty Creek Landfill Construction & Pond Closure

Project overview

Leading field execution and quality.

At AEP–OVEC/IKEC Clifty Creek Station in Madison, Indiana, I serve as Lead Field Engineer and Construction Quality Control Manager for a three-year, $60 million environmental-infrastructure project.

The work encompasses construction of a Type I landfill and closure of the 75-acre West Boiler Ash Pond and 51-acre Leachate Runoff Collection Pond.

RoleLead Field Engineer / CQCM
Scale$60M · 126 pond acres
DatesDec 2025–Present

01

The challenge

Deliver a new Type I landfill while closing two large ash-management ponds—coordinating active field production, grade control, quality requirements, dewatering, materials, cost, and schedule on a multi-year site.

02

My responsibility

I implement plans and specifications, direct field sequencing, coordinate crews and equipment, manage forecasting and material/cost tracking, lead QA/QC corrective action, and develop four field engineers across surveying, safety, reporting, and project controls.

03

Current impact

I designed dewatering ditches that collectively handle more than one million gallons per month while supporting reliable field execution and quality control across the ongoing project.
