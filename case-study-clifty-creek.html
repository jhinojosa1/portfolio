Clifty Creek Landfill Construction & Pond Closure

Project overview

Leading field execution and quality.

At AEP–OVEC/IKEC Clifty Creek Station in Madison, Indiana, I serve as Lead Field Engineer and Construction Quality Control Manager for a three-year, $60 million environmental-infrastructure project.

The work encompasses construction of a Type I landfill and closure of the 75-acre West Boiler Ash Pond and 51-acre Leachate Runoff Collection Pond.

RoleLead Field Engineer / Quality Control Manager
Scale$60M
DatesDec 2025–Present

01

The challenge

Deliver a new Type I landfill while closing two large ash-management ponds—coordinating active field production, grade control, quality requirements, dewatering, materials, cost, and schedule on a multi-year site.

02

My responsibility

I direct field personnel and equipment, lead inspections, corrective work, and technical submittals, and develop four field engineers across surveying, material tracking, safety, reporting, and CAD.

03

Engineering impact

I designed quicklime soil-solidification plans covering 20 acres, Portland-cement soil-solidification plans covering 15 acres, and dewatering ditches handling more than one million gallons per month.

04

Commercial contribution

I supported a successful bid for an additional $2 million contract scope by identifying and negotiating with vendors for erosion-control materials, seeding, and equipment.
